It feels like only big companies have the resources nessecary to report on sustainability. Either way, you probably represent a small-medium sized business interested in commencing sustainability reporting.


A recurring comment is "where do I even beging reporting on sustainability?".

Your sustainability report is a non-financial report on your company's emissions, strategy, stakeholder awareness, risks, economics and social awareness. You collect data from a variety of sources which - when compiled can work as a guideline for improvement and awareness.


Your company has stake holders, which consist of management, employers, customers, service providers and other interest groups. These stake holders are interested in your company's climate values, economic impact, future climate risk, current actions, opportunities and works as a way to insure those stake holders that you are doing the work.

Your current sustainability report will work as documentation, while the 'green change' progresses. Wether or not your current actions are positive or negative, it is more important that your start documenting. Why?


Take Europe and its new Green Deal. The green deal will work as a classification system, in order to objectively assess company's climate actions. Your stake holders are searching for, actively assessing and make decisions based on sustainable actions taken by your company. Conducting a sustainability report provides transaparency for your stake holders, and provide you with data which decisions can be based on.
