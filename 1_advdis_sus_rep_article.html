An overview

Your company has stake holders, which consist of management, employers, customers, service providers and other interest groups. These stake holders are interested in your company's climate values, economic impact, future climate risk, current actions, opportunities and works as a way to insure those stake holders that you are doing the work.

Your current sustainability report will work as documentation, while the 'green change' progresses. Wether or not your current actions are positive or negative, it is more important that your start documenting. Why?

Take Europe and its new Green Deal..

The green deal will work as a classification system, in order to objectively assess company's climate actions. Your stake holders are searching for, actively assessing and make decisions based on sustainable actions taken by your company.

Conducting a sustainability report provides transaparency for your stake holders, and provide you with data which decisions can be based on.

Disadvantages of sustainability reporting

Which reporting-standard to use?

We are all missing is a standardized sustainability reporting standard. But, your most important activity now is to start.

Internationally you have the recognized certificate called ISO 14000, which is "a set of standards created to help companies around the world reduce their adverse impact on the environment". This is a huge and broad topic, with many specific "sub-categories" within the ISO 14000 series.

Furthermore, withing each nation you have specific certifications which is adapted to national standards. In norway we have a certification called MiljÃ¸fyrtÃ¥rn.

This certification is old and covers a broad set of social, economic and environmental topics which provides you with a sustainability report.

Nation-specific reporting standards will grow stronger going ahead. These standards are implementing sustainability reporting rules and regulations, which makes it easier for you to just 'fill in the blanks'.

You could even break it even further down. An executive decisions on your climate strategy, is in fact a documeneted sustainability effort. Pick a topic on of the 17 UN Goals for Sustainable Development, and do just that! You now have a signed boards decision on sustainability. Sure, it is not a standards - but - it is a start.

Should you make your own emissions report?

A norwegian company I spoke with last year - they build bridges - created their own emissions report (greenhouse gas). By working together with their contractor Innovation Norway, they were able to use EPDs, electricity certificate and basic math to provide the nessecary documentation.

The bridge project they were submitting their emissions report for, was comparing the difference in emissions on a bridge produced in norway and china.

In simple terms. Emissions for producing a bridge in norway, taking iron from sweden and 'green electricity' i.e hydropower from norway - was comparable to the emissions related to transportation of a chinese bridge. This excluded the production of a bridge in china.

Even though the bridge was more expensive, the total emissions related to that specific bridge was lower. This example is meant to give you two things.

You can create your own emissions report if the product you are making, consists of very few elements.

Producing a bridge with robots demands iron and electricity.

Of course you'd have aluminium and other essentials, but since they make up less that 1% of the project's materials - you don't take them into consideration. .

You feel its only those with resources that can report on sustainability?

It does take time, especially since there are few automations available for reporting sustainability.

The vocabulary is new and actually doing the work can be grudging. But you've already documented your board's decision to do something, right?

If you break sustainability reporting up in smaller pieces it can become easier. Let's have a look.

An example is to use a ghg-protocol, which is an internationally approved way of calculating your emissions.

Furthermore, you'd decide to only report on scope 1, which is internal emissions. Your own emissions. There are two parametes to this, stationary and mobile combustion.

For stationary combustion you need: Fuel Type, Fuel Usage and Units for usage (volume or weight).

Let us say you use Mixed Electricity (Type), then 10 (Usage) and kWh (Units).

If you plot this into your GHG Emissions Calculation Tool (free online) - you are already documenting your emissions! Way to go.

You also have Scope 2 and Scope 3, which cover more advanced ways of calculating your emissions - but scope 1 will suffice.

Government institutions make the heavy lifting and will have to come up with financial incentives which makes your transition from a "brown" to "green" business a pleasure.

Benefits of sustainability reporting


You don't know where to start? Look to your accountant for help.

The American Institute of Certified Public Accountants [] view their function on sustainability accounting and reporting as follows: "Members in business, industry and government - can add value within their organizations by serving in an integrative role in the value creation process, linking company strategy to sustainability, evaluating risks and opportunities, and providing measurement, accounting and reportins skills (Arden. 2015, 8-12).

In simple terms. Your accountant knows your company's financials, probably better than anyone else you work with.

Their role is unbiased and give trust to your financial numbers. So why not non-financial numbers? A new framework was released by the Finnish, Swedish and Norwegian national accountant committee called Nordic Sustainability Reporting Standard (NSRS).

A simple - as in practical - standard crafted over six or seven years, the executive Chair Tellefsen told me over the phone. This framework will give you the nessecary tools to start straight away - and its freely available online!

Why should you start reporting now? Trust.

Employees, stakeholders, customers and other businesses rely on your transparency.

With the new introduction of EU's Green Taxsonomy, it will provide a framework and classification on how your company is helping sustainable development.

Even though there are some loose threads in terms of how to practically do their reporting, you'll get left behind if you don't report on actual numbers.

What do I mean with left behind? Say you start reporting from today onwards.

A board meeting will suffice, with an executive order which states you company's climate action going forwards. There are plenty of examples online. What you now have is your first documentation, and it is the documentation your bank, employees, business partners and customers are looking for.

Of course, this is only the first step. But it feels good. You're moving forward. EU's Green Taxonomy is complicated and is meant to 'weed out' greenwashing and give companies like yourself a framework to do the transition.

Disadvantages of Sustainability Reporting

Why should you even start, where's the incentives?

Let's talk money.

If we call green loans to be sustainable, and brown loans as being unsustainable - then there isn't any price incentive just yet. Speaking to your loan provider will let you see this first hand.

Furthermore sustainable innovations, products and services are more expensive for now. Yours will be too.

Public tenders. As compliance becomes clearer, you'll have to report, document and submit your emissions. Municipalities and counties will favor those that have the nessecary documentation, since it'll be weighted in the tender.

In Norway, municipalities weigh climate 30% and price 70%. Starting this process now, will make this easier ahead.

Kick-backs. Documentations such as a signed board document, or a strategic document on sustainability, sustainable certifications such as ISO14001 - or anything which prove your seriousness on climate - is documentation. You've done something.

Being able to prove that your company took measures today, could mean that you're eligible for a financial goodies ahead. Besides, it gives you bragging rights. PR, marketing, advertising or any communication that bolsters your brand - will influence your stake holders. What I'm trying to say. It will take time, resources and money to do the work, but it will pay off.

Financial institutions are looking for 'greener' companies, and will transfer money from brown to green loan portfolios. If you document well and spend the time nessecary to comply, you'll benefit with lower rates, tax breaks and better access to capital.
